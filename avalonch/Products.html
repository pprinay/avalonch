SCENARIO · HIGH-VALUE THREAT NETWORK

Multi-domain command for a lawful joint operation.

Valley connects widely used defense equipment such as MQ-9 Reaper ISR aircraft, RQ-20 Puma / RQ-11 Raven tactical drones, Black Hornet nano-drones, UH-60 Black Hawk / MH-60 Seahawk helicopters, Stryker / JLTV / MRAP vehicles, PackBot / TALON ground robots, patrol boats, radar feeds, EO/IR cameras, satellite imagery, SATCOM, and ATAK-style tactical maps into one command layer.

  • Instead of every asset running in a separate software system, Valley builds one live operational picture across air, land, sea, space, and ground teams.
  • A commander could give one instruction: "Track the convoy, monitor the vessel, avoid civilian areas, and keep response units ready."
  • Valley would task the MQ-9 for wide-area surveillance, Puma/Raven for route monitoring, Black Hornet for close building reconnaissance, patrol boats for maritime tracking, PackBot/TALON for hazardous inspection, and helicopters or ground vehicles for human-authorized response.
  • The result is faster coordination, lower operator workload, better situational awareness, and a unified command chain for mixed manned and unmanned systems.

↳ Valley would not replace the equipment. It would make the equipment act like one coordinated force instead of ten disconnected systems.

SCENARIO · BURNING-BUILDING RESCUE

Unified incident command for emergency response.

Valley connects common emergency-response equipment such as DJI Matrice 300/350 RTK, Skydio X10, thermal cameras, firefighter SCBA systems, helmet cameras, body-worn location tags, gas detectors, building CCTV, fire-panel data, ladder trucks, ambulances, police traffic units, utility shutoff systems, and indoor robots like PackBot / TALON.

  • In a burning-building rescue, Valley creates one live 3D map of smoke, heat, blocked routes, firefighter location, victim location, oxygen levels, and structural risk.
  • The incident commander could give one instruction: "Find the trapped person and identify the safest extraction route."
  • Valley would send the thermal drone to scan windows and rooftops, direct an indoor robot through smoke-filled hallways, pull building floor plans and sensor data, guide firefighters to the safest stairwell, position the ladder truck, notify EMS, and coordinate police road clearance.
  • The result is faster victim location, safer firefighter entry, better multi-agency coordination, and a higher chance of saving lives in dangerous environments.

↳ For civil missions, the value is not combat — it is faster search, safer firefighter entry, better multi-agency coordination, and a live 3D picture of a dangerous environment.
